Thruline is an app that let's you explore the news like never before. Unlike other apps that only try to deliver stories that you already know you like, thruline aims to give you what you will like — even before you know it!

There are thousands of daily newspapers in the United states, and even more online-only news and opinion sites. Yet most people only skim a handful of the top sites. Thruline aims to give you a greater diversity of content, so that you can find the hidden gems. But that's not all! As you use thruline, it constantly tests and challenges you, presenting news stories and opinions pieces from different perspective than your own, whether that be ideological or geographical. But for those of you worried that you'll be inundated with articles and blog posts from The Daily Caller (if you're liberal), fret not! Thruline is smart enough to give you articles that you'll be receptive to. Think more David Brooks and less Charles Krauthammer.

FAQ

Who should use thruline?
Anyone who wants a more immersive, serendipitous, and challenging content-consuming experience. Anyone tired of the bubbles and echo chambers that form online and wants to break through — and get a more diverse and comprehensive look at the news.
How does it work?
After answering a few questions to gauge your general proclivities, thruline will present you with content — presented in beautiful and visually appealing form — designed to satisfy both what you like, and what you might like. Occasionally you'll see something that isn't what you would normally seek out yourself, something designed to challenge you. This might be a news article from a foreign publication on a topic of relevance to the U.S. but lightly covered by U.S. news media. It might be an op-ed by a moderate conservative (if you skew liberal) or a moderate liberal (if you skew conservative). Depending on what you read and how you rate the things you read, thruline will adjust itself so that it can present you with better matches in the future.
